The COVID-19 pandemic, also known as the coronavirus pandemic,
is an ongoing pandemic of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID‑19),
caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS‑CoV‑2).
The outbreak was first identified in Wuhan, China, in
December 2019. The World Health Organization declared the outbreak
a public health emergency of international concern on 30 January,
and a pandemic on 11 March. As of 27 May 2020, more than 5.61
million cases of COVID-19 have been
reported in more than 188 countries and territories, resulting
in more than 350,000 deaths. More than 5 million people have
recovered from the virus.
